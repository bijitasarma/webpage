Quantum Computing and Quantum Technology

We explore and engineer the quantum world across scales, from photons and phonons to superconducting circuits, to build the next generation of intelligent quantum technologies. Our vision is to push quantum computing, sensing, and metrology from theory to real devices by combining cutting‑edge quantum control, error correction, and superconducting architectures with powerful machine‑learning tools. We design fast, high‑fidelity quantum gates, variational and simulation algorithms, and quantum‑enhanced protocols, while developing machine learning for quantum and quantum machine learning side by side. At the same time, we remain deeply rooted in fundamental quantum physics: quantum optics, optomechanics, magnonics, and cavity QED, where ideas like unconventional blockade, ground‑state cooling, and hybrid opto‑magno‑mechanical platforms inspire new interdisciplinary ways to sense, process, and harness information from a distinctly quantum angle.

Focus Areas

Quantum Error Correction

RL-powered pulse shaping and continuous-time protocols for robust, fault-tolerant operations on noisy superconducting processors.

Quantum Control & Gates

Fast, high-fidelity gate design in multilevel superconducting circuits using optimal control and deep reinforcement learning.

Quantum Algorithms & QML

Variational optimization, quantum simulations, and quantum machine learning architectures tailored to near-term hardware.

Quantum Sensing & Metrology

Quantum-enhanced sensing protocols and precision metrology using hybrid opto–magno–mechanical and cavity-QED platforms.

Superconducting Circuits

Design and control of multiqubit superconducting architectures, couplers, and fluxonium/transmon devices for scalable quantum processors.

Quantum Optics & Optomechanics

Nonclassical light and mechanics, ground-state cooling, and blockade phenomena in cavity optomechanical and related platforms.

Magnonics & Cavity QED

Hybrid magnon–photon–phonon systems for quantum information processing, sensing, and cavity-QED-inspired quantum technologies.

Research Areas

Quantum Error Correction

We utilize measurement-based feedback strategies and continuous-time protocols for Quantum Error Correction (QEC). We explore autonomous QEC schemes powered by RL-pulse shaping for robust fault-tolerant operations.

Quantum Control & Gates

Designing fast and high-fidelity quantum gates for superconducting qubits using machine learning ansatz and deep reinforcement learning, targeting multilevel superconducting circuits.

Quantum Algorithms & QML

Variational optimization, quantum simulations, and the development of quantum machine learning architectures tailored directly to the capabilities of near-term hardware.

Quantum Sensing & Metrology

Quantum-enhanced sensing protocols and precision metrology utilizing continuous observation, along with hybrid opto–magno–mechanical and cavity-QED platforms.

Superconducting Circuits

Design and advanced control of multiqubit superconducting architectures, incorporating tunable couplers and fluxonium/transmon devices for scalable processors.

Quantum Optics & Optomechanics

Investigating nonclassical light and mechanics, exploring ground-state cooling, photon antibunching, and blockade phenomena in cavity optomechanical systems.

Magnonics & Cavity QED

Studying hybrid magnon–photon–phonon systems to push boundaries in quantum information processing, sensing, and cavity-QED-inspired quantum technologies.
